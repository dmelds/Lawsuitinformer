Hernia Mesh Product List

This page gives a broad overview of hernia mesh products and manufacturers often discussed when people research hernia mesh complications and lawsuits.

Hernia mesh is sold in many forms and under many model names. Some products are made from synthetic materials, some include absorbable components, and some use coatings intended to reduce adhesion or improve placement. Product naming can be confusing, especially years after surgery.

This list is not a complete catalog of every hernia mesh ever sold. It is a general overview of products and product families that people often encounter when looking into hernia mesh issues.

Why product identification matters

In hernia mesh claims, identifying the exact product can matter because different models may have different construction, coating systems, indications, and alleged failure patterns. A person may not remember the mesh name, but the operative report or implant record may contain that information.

Mesh types people may see in records

  • Polypropylene mesh
  • Composite mesh
  • Coated mesh
  • Absorbable or partially absorbable mesh
  • Biologic mesh
  • Hybrid mesh
  • Patch systems
  • Plug systems

Common reasons people research specific hernia mesh products

Many people start by searching for the brand or model after they experience pain, recurrence, infection, bowel complications, or revision surgery. Others only discover the product later when they request hospital records or talk to a lawyer reviewing the case.
