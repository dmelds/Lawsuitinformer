Hernia Mesh Product List

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: March 2026

This hernia mesh product list provides a broad overview of manufacturers and product names often researched by people looking into hernia mesh complications, product identification, and related lawsuits.

Important: This page provides general educational information and does not constitute legal advice.

Why people look up hernia mesh product names

Many people do not know the exact mesh product used in their surgery until they review hospital records or operative reports. Once complications arise, identifying the product may become an important step in understanding the medical history and researching whether the device has been discussed in litigation.

Why product identification can matter

Different hernia mesh products may have different materials, coatings, pore structures, shapes, fixation systems, or absorbable components. Because of that, the exact model used in a surgery can matter when reviewing complications, recurrence, revision procedures, and possible legal questions.

Types of hernia mesh people may see in records

  • Polypropylene mesh
  • Composite mesh
  • Coated mesh
  • Absorbable or partially absorbable mesh
  • Biologic mesh
  • Hybrid mesh
  • Patch systems
  • Plug systems

What records may help identify the product

People often begin with the operative report from the original hernia repair, hospital billing records, implant logs, or later revision surgery records. In some cases, the specific product name appears clearly in the chart. In others, it takes more digging to determine which device was used.
