Hair Relaxer Litigation

Hair Relaxer Lawsuit Updates

This page tracks recent developments in the federal hair relaxer litigation, including major rulings, MDL growth, and where the case appears to stand now.

Last updated: April 4, 2026

Where the Hair Relaxer MDL Stands Now

The federal hair relaxer litigation remains one of the largest active product liability MDLs in the country. As of April 1, 2026, the Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation reported 11,371 pending actions in MDL No. 3060, In re: Hair Relaxer Marketing, Sales Practices, and Products Liability Litigation.

The litigation is pending before Judge Mary M. Rowland in the Northern District of Illinois. The broad issues still center on whether certain chemical hair relaxer products were defectively designed, whether consumers were adequately warned, and whether the products are linked to cancers and other alleged injuries.

Recent Hair Relaxer MDL Developments

April 2026: More Than 11,000 Cases Are Pending in the MDL

The latest JPML statistics report shows that the hair relaxer MDL had 11,371 pending actions as of April 1, 2026. That keeps it among the largest active multidistrict litigations in the federal system.

Late 2025 Into 2026: Bellwether Track Continues to Move Forward

Public reporting indicates that 32 bellwether cases had been selected by May 2025, with plaintiffs and defendants each choosing 16 cases. Discovery in those cases has been underway, and commentary on the MDL has indicated that early bellwether trials do not appear likely before 2027.

October 2024: Judge Limited Common Benefit Fees to MDL Recoveries

In a closely watched ruling, Judge Rowland declined to extend common benefit fee holdbacks to cases outside the MDL. Reuters reported that the court kept the fee structure for MDL recoveries but concluded it could not impose those fees on state court cases or unfiled claims that were not actually before the court.

September 2024: Consumer Reimbursement Class Claims Survived Dismissal

A related consumer class action also survived a major dismissal challenge. Reuters reported that the court largely rejected efforts by product makers to dismiss claims seeking reimbursement, punitive damages, and certain medical monitoring relief tied to purchases of hair relaxer products.

Earlier Rulings Allowed the Core Injury Litigation to Continue

The hair relaxer litigation cleared an important early hurdle when the court allowed the core injury allegations to move forward instead of dismissing them at the pleading stage. That helped keep the MDL on track for broader discovery and bellwether preparation.

What These Updates May Mean

At this stage, the hair relaxer litigation still appears to be in the evidence-building and case-development phase rather than the settlement phase. Large case counts alone do not guarantee results for any individual claimant, but they do show that the litigation continues to attract filings and remains active.

The bellwether process matters because those early cases often help shape how both sides evaluate expert evidence, settlement pressure, and trial risk. Until that process moves further along, the most important developments are usually court rulings, discovery disputes, case selection decisions, and the overall pace of the MDL.
