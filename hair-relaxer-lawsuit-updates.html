Where the Hair Relaxer MDL Stands Now

The federal hair relaxer litigation remains one of the largest active multidistrict litigations in the country. Public court reporting has shown that thousands of cases have been centralized in the proceeding, and the litigation continues to move through discovery, case management, and bellwether preparation.

The broader issues in this litigation continue to focus on product design, product warnings, chemical exposure allegations, scientific causation questions, and whether manufacturers adequately informed consumers about alleged risks associated with repeated product use.

Hair relaxer claims are still being filed. If you used hair relaxer products and were later diagnosed with uterine, endometrial, or ovarian cancer, fibroids, or endometriosis, you may qualify for a free case review.

Recent Hair Relaxer MDL Developments

April 2026:

The hair relaxer MDL continues to rank among the largest active product liability proceedings in federal court, with more than 11,000 pending actions reported in public MDL statistics.

Late 2025 into 2026:

The bellwether track has continued to move forward, with selected cases proceeding through discovery and early case-development work.

2024 to 2025:

Important rulings in the litigation addressed issues such as fee structure, consumer reimbursement claims, and whether major parts of the litigation could continue moving forward.

Why These Updates Matter

In large coordinated proceedings like this one, updates can matter because they show whether the litigation is still expanding, whether major motions are being denied or granted, and whether the case is moving closer to bellwether trials or other major turning points.

That does not mean any update predicts the outcome of an individual claim. But readers often follow these developments to understand whether the litigation appears active, whether court deadlines are advancing, and whether the case is entering a more developed phase.

What People Often Watch for Next

  • Growth or slowdown in the overall number of filed cases
  • Bellwether case selection and discovery progress
  • Rulings on expert evidence and scientific causation issues
  • Case management orders that affect the pace of the litigation
  • Whether the MDL appears to move closer to trial preparation

Why Bellwether Progress Gets Attention

Bellwether cases are often watched closely because they can help both sides evaluate evidence, expert testimony, trial themes, and overall litigation risk. In many MDLs, those early track cases can influence how the broader proceeding develops.

Readers following hair relaxer litigation updates are often trying to understand whether the case is still in early development, whether it is moving into a more mature stage, or whether major rulings suggest the litigation is gaining momentum.
