Chemical Exposure Lawsuits

Some lawsuits involve allegations that exposure to toxic chemicals, industrial pollution, pesticides, or contaminated water contributed to serious health conditions.

How Chemical Exposure Lawsuits Arise

Chemical exposure lawsuits may arise when individuals believe that toxic substances released into the environment, workplaces, or consumer products contributed to health problems.

These cases sometimes involve contaminated water, pesticides, industrial chemicals, or defective products.

Examples of Chemical Exposure Lawsuits

PFAS Water Contamination

Cases involving contamination of drinking water by PFAS chemicals.

Roundup Cancer Lawsuits

Litigation involving glyphosate herbicide exposure.

Paraquat Parkinson's Lawsuits

Cases involving allegations that pesticide exposure may contribute to Parkinson's disease.

Health Conditions Linked to Chemical Exposure

  • Kidney cancer
  • Testicular cancer
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Immune system effects
