Environmental Contamination Lawsuits

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: March 19, 2026

Environmental contamination lawsuits involve claims that communities, workers, or families were exposed to hazardous chemicals through polluted drinking water, industrial waste, contaminated soil, landfill runoff, pesticide drift, or toxic emissions released into the environment. These cases can involve large groups of people and sometimes develop into coordinated litigation or mass tort proceedings.

Important: This page provides general educational information about environmental contamination litigation and does not constitute medical or legal advice.

Why environmental contamination lawsuits are filed

These lawsuits are often filed when people allege that a company, industrial operation, government-related site, landfill, military facility, or other source released harmful substances into the surrounding environment. Claims may involve groundwater contamination, polluted drinking water, chemical runoff, toxic air emissions, or other alleged exposure pathways affecting nearby communities.

  • Industrial chemical exposure claims
  • Soil contamination and landfill pollution claims
  • Toxic air emission allegations
  • Community exposure and neighborhood contamination disputes
  • Claims involving long-term environmental cleanup failures

Why these cases can be complex

Environmental contamination cases often involve difficult questions about where contamination came from, how long exposure lasted, what substances were present, whether public warnings were given, and how an illness or injury may relate to the alleged contamination. These cases may require environmental testing, residential history, medical records, expert testimony, and evidence about corporate or government knowledge of contamination.
