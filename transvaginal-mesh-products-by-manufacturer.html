Transvaginal Mesh Manufacturers and Public Product Identifiers

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: March 2026

Many people researching transvaginal mesh complications want to know which manufacturer made a product, what the product was called, and whether any public product identifiers exist. This page is designed as a public-reference guide listing known manufacturers, product families, and selected public FDA identifiers such as 510(k) numbers, catalog numbers, model numbers, and recall references where available.

Important: This page provides general educational information and does not constitute legal advice.

Why product identification matters

In transvaginal mesh cases, product identification can be one of the most important factual issues. People often begin by trying to confirm the manufacturer, product name, and any device references that may appear in public records or medical records. In many situations, operative reports, implant stickers, hospital billing records, and revision surgery records may be more specific than public databases alone.

Important limitation on model and lot numbers

Public records do not provide one complete master list of every transvaginal mesh product together with every model number and every lot number ever used. Some public FDA records identify a product family clearly. Some recall records identify catalog or model numbers. Some recall entries also include batch or lot references. But those identifiers are not consistently available across all products and all manufacturers.

For that reason, this page is written as a guide to publicly identifiable products rather than a guarantee of a complete lot-number database.

Ethicon / Johnson & Johnson

Ethicon products frequently discussed in transvaginal mesh litigation include the GYNECARE PROLIFT family and certain TVT products.

  • GYNECARE PROLIFT +M Pelvic Floor Repair Systems
  • Tension Free Vaginal Tape (TVT) System
  • GYNECARE TVT Obturator System

Public FDA records identify GYNECARE PROLIFT under a 510(k) clearance and describe it as a pelvic floor repair system. Some public adverse-event records also include catalog references for specific product variants, but those entries should be understood as record-specific identifiers rather than a complete public catalog.

Boston Scientific

Boston Scientific products discussed in public mesh records include the Pinnacle pelvic floor repair kits and certain later pelvic floor support systems.

  • Pinnacle Pelvic Floor Repair Kits
  • Uphold and Uphold LITE product lines
  • Xenform-related pelvic floor products

Public FDA materials identify Pinnacle pelvic floor repair mesh in 510(k) records, and public recall records show that at least some Pinnacle products were associated with model and batch references. That is useful for research, but it still does not create a complete public lot-number list for every Boston Scientific mesh device.

C.R. Bard

Bard products commonly referenced in public records include the Avaulta product line.

  • Avaulta Solo Synthetic Support System
  • Avaulta Plus Biosynthetic Support System

Public FDA records identify these Avaulta systems in 510(k) documentation. Bard mesh products for pelvic organ prolapse were also identified in later market-withdrawal notices outside the original clearance records.

American Medical Systems (AMS)

Public FDA materials identify AMS pelvic floor repair systems including:

  • Apogee System
  • Perigee System

These systems appear in public FDA 510(k) documentation involving pelvic floor repair kits and related device descriptions.

Coloplast

Coloplast products that appear in public mesh records include the Restorelle DirectFix line.

  • Restorelle DirectFix Anterior
  • Restorelle DirectFix Posterior Mesh

Public records identify Restorelle DirectFix products in both regulatory and recall materials. Some entries include catalog-level references, but availability of model, batch, or lot information remains uneven.

Examples of public identifiers people may find

  • Manufacturer name
  • Product family name
  • 510(k) number
  • Catalog number
  • Model number
  • Batch or lot number in some recalls

What records are usually more helpful than public databases?

Anyone trying to identify the exact device used in a particular surgery will often need to look beyond public databases. The records that may be most helpful include:

  • Operative reports
  • Implant stickers or implant logs
  • Hospital billing records
  • Surgeon records
  • Revision surgery records
  • Pathology or explant documentation

Public databases may help confirm that a product existed or that a model or catalog reference appears in a public record. But they often do not replace the patient’s own medical records when exact product identification matters.
