How Long Do Lawsuits Take?

The length of a lawsuit can vary widely depending on the type of case, the court system, and whether the case settles or goes to trial. Some lawsuits resolve within a few months, while others can take several years.

Average Timeline for a Lawsuit

Many civil lawsuits take between one and three years from the time the case is filed to a final resolution. However, some cases may resolve faster if a settlement is reached early in the process.

Factors That Affect How Long a Lawsuit Takes

Several factors influence how long a legal case may take, including the complexity of the case, the amount of evidence involved, and the schedule of the court handling the case.

Settlement vs Trial

Many lawsuits end in settlement before reaching trial. When both parties agree to settle, the case may resolve much faster than if it proceeds through the entire court process.
