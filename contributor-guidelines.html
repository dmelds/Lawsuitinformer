Attorney-led legal education

Contributor Guidelines

Lawsuit Informer features short educational insights from attorneys, medical professionals, scientific reviewers, and other professionals who follow lawsuits, toxic exposure, product liability, and consumer safety issues.

If you work in one of these areas and want to share perspective on a public development, we'd like to hear from you.

What we publish

Short contributions, usually one of these:

  • A quoted insight of 100–300 words on a public development
  • A brief Q&A of three to five questions
  • Commentary on litigation, regulatory, or safety news
  • A short explainer that helps readers understand a topic

How it works

You send your insight in your own words. We edit for length and clarity. You approve the final version before it’s published. Contributions are credited with your name, title, firm or company, and a profile link.

Ground rules

Contributions should be educational and based on public information or professional experience. We don’t publish confidential information, case-specific legal advice, or promotional content. If a contributor’s firm participates on Lawsuit Center in the same litigation area, we’ll note that on the piece.

Interested in contributing?

If you are an attorney, medical professional, scientific reviewer, litigation-support professional, or other qualified contributor interested in providing educational insight, please get in touch.
