About Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: April 21, 2026

Lawsuit Informer is an attorney-led educational site that explains lawsuits, toxic exposure, and consumer safety issues in plain English — written for readers trying to understand their situation before they contact a lawyer.

Important:

Lawsuit Informer provides general educational information only and does not offer legal advice through this website.

Founded by David Meldofsky

California-licensed attorney (CA Bar No. 263673) · Founder of Lawsuit Informer

Lawsuit Informer was founded by David Meldofsky to make complex legal topics easier to understand. The site grew out of a simple observation: most people researching a potential claim hit dense legal jargon long before they find anything useful.

Every guide on the site is written with that reader in mind — someone who wants to understand how lawsuits actually work, what evidence matters, and what questions to ask, before they decide whether to speak with a lawyer.

Our Mission

To give people researching a potential lawsuit clear, accurate, plain-English information about how the legal process works — so they can make informed decisions about what to do next.

What We Cover

Lawsuit Informer focuses on:

  • How lawsuits work and core legal process guides
  • Mass tort and class action topics
  • Toxic exposure and environmental contamination
  • Product liability and consumer safety issues
  • Illnesses, symptoms, and health issues people research alongside lawsuits
  • Common legal research questions related to injuries, exposure, and risk

You can explore more through our Legal Guides, Browse Lawsuits, Illnesses, and Symptoms hubs.

Part of The Lawsuit Network

Lawsuit Informer is one of two public sites that make up The Lawsuit Network:

  • Lawsuit Informer (this site) — educational content explaining how lawsuits work, key legal concepts, and consumer safety issues.
  • Lawsuit Center — a searchable directory of active lawsuits and the law firms investigating them, for readers looking to connect with counsel.

Keeping the two separate lets each site do its job well: education stays educational, and the directory handles the work of matching readers to firms handling specific types of claims.

Editorial Approach

Content is written to explain legal concepts clearly and accurately for readers without a legal background, with a focus on the questions people actually have before they speak with a lawyer. The site is led by a California-licensed attorney and reviewed against a published Editorial Policy.

Lawsuit Informer also publishes Professor Perspective, a section featuring academic commentary from law professors and legal academics on litigation, legal issues, and public legal education.

Not Legal Advice

The information on Lawsuit Informer is for educational purposes only. It is not legal advice, and no attorney-client relationship is formed through use of this site. Reading content, submitting information through a form, or contacting the site does not by itself create such a relationship.

For advice about a specific situation, consult a qualified attorney.
