What Happens If You Ignore a Lawsuit?

Last updated: March 2026

Ignoring a lawsuit can lead to serious legal consequences. When someone fails to respond within the required timeframe, the court may move forward without that person’s participation.

Important: This page provides general educational information only and does not constitute legal advice. Laws and court procedures vary by jurisdiction.

Default Judgment

If the defendant does not respond, the court may issue a default judgment in favor of the person who filed the lawsuit. This means the case may be decided without the defendant presenting a defense.

Financial Consequences

A default judgment may allow the winning party to pursue collection efforts such as wage garnishment, bank levies, or other attempts to recover money depending on the situation and local law.

Loss of Legal Defenses

By ignoring the lawsuit, the defendant may lose the opportunity to present evidence, challenge claims, or raise legal defenses in court.

Why Responding Matters

Responding to a lawsuit allows both sides to present their case and helps ensure the legal process moves forward fairly. Even when a person disagrees with the claims, ignoring court papers can make the outcome far worse.

Common Reasons People Ignore Lawsuits

  • They do not understand the deadline to respond
  • They assume the lawsuit is not serious
  • They feel overwhelmed or confused by the paperwork
  • They believe they can deal with it later
