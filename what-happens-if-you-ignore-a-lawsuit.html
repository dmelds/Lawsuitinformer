What Happens If You Ignore a Lawsuit?

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: April 2, 2026

Ignoring a lawsuit can lead to serious consequences. When someone does not respond within the required time, the court may move forward without that person’s participation. That can make the situation much harder to fix later.

Why Ignoring a Lawsuit Can Be Serious

When a lawsuit is filed, the defendant is usually given a deadline to respond. If that deadline passes without a response, the other side may ask the court to proceed without hearing the defendant’s side. In many situations, that can lead to a default judgment.

Even if a person believes the lawsuit is unfair, mistaken, or exaggerated, ignoring the papers can make the result worse than responding and participating in the process.

What Is a Default Judgment?

A default judgment is a court decision entered after the defendant fails to respond or appear as required. In general, this means the person who filed the lawsuit may win without the defendant presenting evidence, raising defenses, or challenging the claims in court.

Possible Financial Consequences

If a default judgment is entered, the winning party may try to collect on that judgment. Depending on the facts and local law, collection efforts may include wage garnishment, bank levies, property liens, or other legal steps to recover money.

Loss of the Chance to Defend the Case

Ignoring a lawsuit may mean losing the opportunity to dispute the allegations, present documents, raise defenses, challenge damages, or explain what actually happened. Once a case moves forward without a response, fixing the problem later can be more difficult and may require extra court filings.

Why People Sometimes Ignore Lawsuits

  • They do not understand that the papers require a response
  • They assume the lawsuit is not serious
  • They feel overwhelmed by the paperwork
  • They believe they can deal with it later
  • They are confused about deadlines or court procedure

Why Responding Matters

Responding to a lawsuit helps preserve the ability to defend the case. It gives both sides the chance to present their position and helps ensure the court hears more than one side of the dispute. Even when someone disagrees strongly with the claims, participating is usually far better than ignoring the case.

Can the Situation Be Fixed Later?

In some situations, courts may allow a default to be challenged or set aside, but that does not happen automatically. It usually requires additional steps, explanations, and timing considerations. That is one reason responding early matters so much.

Frequently Asked Questions About Ignoring a Lawsuit

What happens if you ignore a lawsuit?

Ignoring a lawsuit can allow the court to move forward without hearing your side. If the response deadline passes, the plaintiff may ask the court for a default judgment.

What is a default judgment?

A default judgment is a court decision entered after a defendant fails to respond or appear as required. It may allow the plaintiff to win without the defendant presenting evidence or defenses.

Can money be collected after a default judgment?

Depending on the law and facts, a judgment creditor may try to collect through legal tools such as wage garnishment, bank levies, property liens, or other collection procedures.

Can a default judgment be challenged later?

In some situations, a person may ask the court to set aside a default or default judgment, but it is not automatic. The person usually must act quickly and provide a legally acceptable reason.

Why is ignoring court papers risky?

Ignoring court papers is risky because it may cause missed deadlines, loss of the chance to raise defenses, and possible judgment collection efforts. Responding on time is usually easier than trying to fix a default later.

What should someone do after receiving lawsuit papers?

A person who receives lawsuit papers should read them carefully, note any deadlines, keep copies, and consider contacting a lawyer or the court clerk for procedural information. Court rules and deadlines vary by jurisdiction.

Does ignoring a lawsuit make it go away?

No. Ignoring a lawsuit usually does not make it go away. The court may continue the case without the defendant's participation, which can make the outcome worse and harder to correct later.

Is a default judgment the end of the case?

A default judgment can be a major turning point, but the exact effect depends on the court, the type of case, and local rules. In some situations, additional collection or enforcement steps may follow.
