New Mexico Verdict Against Meta

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: April 2026

A New Mexico jury returned a major verdict against Meta in a state case involving alleged misleading safety statements and harms affecting young users. The reported award was $375 million, making this one of the most closely watched recent outcomes involving a major social media company.

Important: This page provides general educational information and does not constitute legal advice.

What is the New Mexico Meta case about?

The New Mexico case is a state lawsuit against Meta focused on platform safety and alleged deceptive practices. The case centered on claims that Meta misled users about safety on its platforms and failed to adequately address serious risks affecting minors.

Why people are paying attention to this verdict

The reported $375 million jury award drew immediate attention because it represents a major courtroom result against a social media company. It also stands out because the case was brought by a state attorney general rather than only by a private individual plaintiff.

How this relates to social media addiction lawsuits

This case is related to the broader wave of lawsuits involving social media and youth harm, but it is not exactly the same as a standard addictive design case. Many social media addiction lawsuits focus on allegations that platform features were designed to keep young users engaged in harmful ways. The New Mexico case involved a different legal path tied to state consumer protection law and platform safety representations.

What the verdict may mean

The verdict may increase pressure on social media companies in future litigation involving youth mental health, platform design, and child safety. It may also encourage closer attention to whether courts will allow states to pursue broader remedies beyond individual injury claims.

What may happen next

Major verdicts against large companies are often followed by appeals and additional proceedings. Reports also indicated that another phase of the New Mexico case was expected in May 2026, with possible requests for further relief related to platform practices.
