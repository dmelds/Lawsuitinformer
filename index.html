Frequently Asked

Common questions

How do I know if I have a lawsuit?

Whether a situation supports a legal claim depends on the facts, the applicable law, and the evidence available. Our guides explain how lawsuits typically work and what kinds of evidence matter. Speaking with a licensed attorney is the only way to evaluate a specific situation.

Does it cost money to research a potential lawsuit?

Reading legal guides and researching lawsuit topics is free. Many lawyers who handle injury, toxic exposure, and product liability cases work on contingency, meaning no fee unless the case succeeds. Policies vary by firm.

What is a mass tort, and how is it different from a class action?

A mass tort involves many individual claims, usually against the same defendant, coordinated together because they share common issues. Unlike a class action, each plaintiff keeps a separate case and receives an individual outcome.

How long do I have to file a lawsuit?

Every state sets deadlines, called statutes of limitations, for filing different types of claims. These vary widely by claim type and jurisdiction. Missing a deadline can bar a case entirely, so timing often matters.
