MEET THE FOUNDER

Attorney-Led, with a Specific View on Why This Site Exists

Lawsuit Informer was founded by David Meldofsky, a California-licensed attorney, because the public-facing legal internet has become difficult to read honestly. Lawsuit pages routinely mix education, advertising, and case intake without signaling which is which, and AI-generated content has made the line harder to find.

The site is the response: pages written or reviewed by a licensed attorney, with visible authorship, dated review notes, and editorial work kept separate from case intake.

David's writing has been published in Attorney at Law Magazine, Law360, and the Daily Journal, with a focus on PFAS regulation, drinking water standards, and consumer-facing legal content.

How Lawsuit Informer Works

The site is built around a few specific commitments — not generic claims about quality.

Written or Reviewed by an Attorney

Every substantive page is written or reviewed by David Meldofsky, a California-licensed attorney, before publication. The site does not publish anonymous content.

Editorial Separated from Intake

Lawsuitinformer.com is the editorial site. Case intake happens on a separate site, lawsuit.center. Calls-to-action are clearly labeled, and editorial decisions aren't driven by intake partners.

Dated Review on Every Page

Substantive pages carry a "last updated" date. Pages on developing topics — PFAS, AFFF, AI litigation — are reviewed on a rolling basis as regulations and case law change.

Disclosed Use of AI in Drafting

AI tools are used in parts of research and drafting. Every published page is reviewed by a licensed attorney for legal accuracy and framing before going live. Disclosed openly on the Editorial Policy.

Medical Review on Health Content

Medical sections of pages discussing symptoms, illness, or toxic exposure are reviewed for general accuracy by Dr. Thomas A. Hatzilabrou, M.D.

Corrections Welcome

If a page contains a factual error or outdated citation, the corrections form sends it directly to editorial review. Substantive corrections update the page's review date.

Subscribe for updates

Get plain-English updates on lawsuits, toxic exposure issues, consumer safety concerns, and major legal developments.

Lawsuit Informer is an attorney-led editorial site covering lawsuits, toxic exposure, product liability, and consumer safety.
