Talcum Powder Ovarian Cancer Lawsuits

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: March 19, 2026

Talcum powder ovarian cancer lawsuits involve claims that long-term use of certain talc-based products may be associated with ovarian cancer and other product safety concerns. These cases often focus on whether manufacturers adequately warned consumers about alleged risks related to repeated talc use over time.

Important: This page provides general educational information about talcum powder litigation, cancer-related allegations, and product liability issues. It does not constitute medical or legal advice.

What is talcum powder?

Talcum powder is made from talc, a naturally occurring mineral composed primarily of magnesium, silicon, and oxygen. Talc has been used in cosmetic and personal care products because it can absorb moisture and reduce friction.

For many years, talc-based powders were marketed for cosmetic and personal hygiene use, which is one reason long-term exposure patterns became an important issue in litigation.

Why people research talcum powder lawsuits

Some lawsuits allege that long-term use of talc-based products may be associated with ovarian cancer. Plaintiffs often argue that manufacturers knew or should have known about potential risks and failed to provide adequate warnings.

These cases often examine scientific research, product testing history, warning labels, company communications, and the history of consumer product marketing.

Health concerns discussed in these cases

  • Ovarian cancer allegations
  • Long-term cosmetic talc exposure
  • Possible asbestos contamination issues in some talc-related litigation
  • Consumer product safety concerns

Common sources of alleged exposure

  • Cosmetic talcum powder products
  • Body powder and personal hygiene products
  • Long-term repeated use of talc-based powders
  • Personal care product exposure over many years

Types of claims involved

  • Failure to warn about alleged cancer risks
  • Product liability claims
  • Negligence allegations related to product safety testing
  • Failure to disclose alleged health hazards

Why these cases can be complex

Talcum powder lawsuits can involve detailed questions about product history, duration of use, diagnosis, exposure patterns, scientific causation, and expert evidence. Some cases also involve disputes about product composition, contamination issues, and what manufacturers knew at different points in time.
