Illnesses and Exposures Linked to Lawsuits

Some lawsuits involve claims that chemical exposure, environmental contamination, or dangerous products may have contributed to certain illnesses. This page explores common illnesses that appear in litigation and the exposures often discussed in these cases.

Cancers Linked to Chemical Exposure

Kidney Cancer

Some lawsuits involving PFAS contamination have discussed potential links between exposure to certain chemicals and kidney cancer.

Testicular Cancer

PFAS exposure has also been examined in connection with testicular cancer in environmental contamination litigation.

Ovarian Cancer

Some product liability cases involving cosmetic products have explored potential links between chemical exposure and ovarian cancer.

Neurological Conditions

Parkinson's Disease

Some pesticide lawsuits have involved allegations that exposure to certain herbicides may contribute to neurological disease.

Neurological Injury

Environmental exposure cases sometimes involve allegations of neurological effects linked to industrial chemicals.

Environmental Exposure

Contaminated Drinking Water

PFAS contamination lawsuits involve allegations that chemicals entered public water systems and exposed communities.

Pesticide Exposure

Agricultural chemical exposure has been discussed in lawsuits involving herbicides and pesticides.

Industrial Chemical Exposure

Some lawsuits involve exposure to chemicals released from manufacturing facilities or industrial sites.
