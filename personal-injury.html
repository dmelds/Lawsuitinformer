Understanding Personal Injury Lawsuits

Last updated: March 2026

Personal injury lawsuits arise when someone is harmed due to another person's negligence or wrongful actions. These cases often involve accidents, unsafe conditions, defective products, or medical mistakes. Understanding how personal injury claims work can help people protect their rights and recover compensation for medical costs, lost income, and other damages.

Important: Personal injury laws vary by jurisdiction. This page provides general educational information about personal injury claims and does not constitute legal advice.

Common Types of Personal Injury Cases

  • Car accidents
  • Slip and fall accidents
  • Medical malpractice
  • Defective product injuries
  • Workplace accidents

How Personal Injury Lawsuits Work

A personal injury claim typically begins when an injured person files a lawsuit against the party responsible for the harm. The case may involve investigating evidence, gathering medical records, and negotiating settlements between the parties involved.

Some personal injury cases may become part of larger litigation such as mass tort lawsuits or class action lawsuits when many people are harmed by the same product or event.

Compensation in Personal Injury Cases

If the injured person succeeds in their claim, compensation may include medical expenses, lost income, pain and suffering, and other damages related to the injury.
