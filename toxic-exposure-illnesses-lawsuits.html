Illnesses Linked to Toxic Exposure Lawsuits

Certain illnesses and cancers have been linked to environmental contamination, industrial chemicals, pesticides, and defective consumer products. Many of these exposures have led to lawsuits when companies failed to warn the public or properly control dangerous substances.

Below are illnesses that have been associated with toxic exposure and the lawsuits that may be connected to them.

Kidney Cancer

Kidney cancer has been linked to several environmental and occupational exposures, including PFAS contamination and industrial solvents.

  • PFAS water contamination
  • Trichloroethylene (TCE) exposure
  • Industrial chemical exposure

Learn more about PFAS lawsuits

Parkinson’s Disease

Research has linked Parkinson’s disease to exposure to certain pesticides and industrial chemicals.

  • Paraquat pesticide exposure
  • Agricultural chemical exposure

Learn more about Paraquat lawsuits

Non-Hodgkin’s Lymphoma

Non-Hodgkin’s lymphoma has been associated with certain herbicides and chemical exposures.

  • Glyphosate herbicide exposure
  • Agricultural pesticide exposure

Learn more about Roundup lawsuits

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer has been strongly linked to certain environmental exposures, including asbestos fibers and industrial air pollution.

  • Asbestos exposure
  • Radon exposure
  • Industrial air pollution

Learn more about asbestos lawsuits
