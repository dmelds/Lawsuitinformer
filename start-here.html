Start Here: Understanding Lawsuits Step by Step

By David Meldofsky, California-licensed attorney · Founder, Lawsuit Informer

Last updated: March 2026

If you are new to lawsuits and trying to understand where to begin, this page is a simple starting point. It is designed to help readers understand the basic process, common legal terms, and the main educational pages that explain how lawsuits are commonly discussed.

Important: This page provides general educational information and does not constitute legal advice.

What is this page for?

Lawsuit topics can feel overwhelming when you are seeing unfamiliar legal terms, case types, and lawsuit categories all at once. This page is meant to simplify that experience by giving you a clear path through the site.

Instead of jumping between unrelated pages, you can use this guide as a central starting point for understanding how lawsuits work and where to go next based on the topic you are researching.

Step 1: Learn how lawsuits work

A strong first step is understanding the basic structure of a lawsuit. That includes how a claim may begin, what lawyers often review early on, why investigations matter, and how cases may proceed through filing, negotiation, or trial.

Read: How Lawsuits Work

Step 2: Understand key legal terms

Many readers are trying to understand the difference between terms like mass tort, class action, settlement, and discovery. These concepts often appear in legal advertising, news coverage, and lawsuit discussions, but they do not all mean the same thing.

  • Class Actions
  • What Is a Settlement?
  • What Is Discovery?

Step 3: Understand how long lawsuits may take

One of the most common questions people have is how long a case may last. The answer depends on the facts, the court process, the number of parties involved, and whether a case settles early or continues through later stages of litigation.

Read: How Long Do Lawsuits Take?

Once you understand the general process, the next step is usually exploring a more specific topic. That may include toxic exposure, chemical exposure, product liability, water contamination, or health issues that people associate with lawsuits.

Why people use a page like this

People often arrive on legal education sites with only part of the picture. Some are trying to understand a diagnosis, some are researching a product or exposure, and others are simply trying to understand whether a lawsuit topic they saw online is real or relevant.

A page like this helps connect those questions to the site’s most useful beginner-friendly resources. It gives readers a place to begin before moving into more detailed pages.
